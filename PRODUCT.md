## Design Context

### Users
Version Watch serves independent developers, startup engineers, technical founders, and platform-minded product teams who need to track meaningful changes across major developer platforms without reading every vendor changelog. Secondary users include agencies, developer advocates, and engineering leads monitoring tooling risk. They use the product to scan important changes quickly, verify official sources, filter by vendor or category, and decide who needs to act.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel precise, confident, high-trust, and disciplined. It should read as a premium intelligence terminal for developers: source-first, practical, spacious, and slightly cinematic on the homepage while staying restrained and efficient on dense utility surfaces.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is a premium developer intelligence surface, not a generic SaaS dashboard, press site, blog, or analytics template. Use a dark, high-contrast theme because the product is built for focused technical scanning. Favor strong layout planes, wide typography, disciplined color, and deliberate hierarchy over decorative cards or repeated stat blocks.

### Design Principles
- Make source verification feel primary, trustworthy, and fast.
- Build dense surfaces for scanning, not spectacle.
- Use homepage motion and atmosphere only where they clarify product value.
- Keep cards structurally necessary, never decorative filler.
- Make the product memorable through precision, hierarchy, and uncommon type/color choices rather than generic gradients or dashboard tropes.
